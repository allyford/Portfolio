Undergraduate Research Assistant
Central Washington University
September 2018 - June 2019

“Shopping while Non-white in Ellensburg, WA” - Presented at CWU SOURCE, May 2019

I designed, conducted, and analyzed a true experiment to compare experiences of minority and white customers at stores in Ellensburg, WA, with Dr. Marte Fallshore. By comparing customer experiences, we were able to conclude that there was no significant difference in treatment of customers based on their assumed race. My goal in this project was to analyze different factors that may affect a customer’s experience with a business. I gained a lot of insight into the importance of considering human diversity when designing a product.

“How Researchers Address Ethical Concerns Regarding Habituating Nonhuman Primates: Results of a Survey and Suggestions for Future Practice”

I analyzed and identified trends in quantitative survey and qualitative interview data for a report on the habituation of primates, with MS Primate Behavior and Ecology student Victoria Green. This was my first experience analyzing data trends and combining quantitative and qualitative data to gain a holistic representation of the status of primate habituation.
